Blackened Salmon with Creamy Mango Salsa and Garlic Parmesan Roasted Asparagus.

Indulge in the rich flavors of our Blackened Salmon, perfectly seared to a crispy crust and served atop a bed of creamy mango salsa. The sweetness of the mango pairs exquisitely with the savory blackening seasoning, while the garlic parmesan roasted asparagus adds a delightful crunch and depth to each bite.

Night-Before Prep-Cooking Checklist for Blackened Salmon with Creamy Mango Salsa and Garlic Parmesan Roasted Asparagus

  • Salmon:
    Prepare marinade by mixing together olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and blackening seasoning. Refrigerate overnight to allow the flavors to meld.
  • Mango Salsa:
    Peel and dice mango, then combine with red onion, jalapeño, cilantro, lime juice, and salt in a bowl. Cover and refrigerate overnight to allow the flavors to meld.
  • Garlic Parmesan Roasted Asparagus:
    Trim asparagus spears and place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with minced garlic, grated parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat evenly. Refrigerate overnight to allow the flavors to meld.
  • Creamy Mango Sauce:
    Combine heavy cream, diced mango, lime juice, honey, and a pinch of salt in a blender or food processor. Blend until smooth, then refrigerate overnight to allow the flavors to meld.
Note: This list assumes that you will be cooking the salmon, roasting the asparagus, and making the creamy mango sauce on the evening of service. The night before, focus on preparing the marinade, mango salsa, garlic parmesan roasted asparagus, and creamy mango sauce to ensure a smooth and efficient service.

Baked Salmon with Saffron Rice Pilaf, Roasted Sweet Potatoes with Harissa Aioli, and Cranberry-Ginger Chutney

Baked Salmon with Saffron Rice Pilaf, Roasted Sweet Potatoes with Harissa Aioli, and Cranberry-Ginger Chutney.

A symphony of flavors and textures, this dish presents a perfectly baked salmon fillet atop a bed of fluffy saffron rice pilaf. Roasted sweet potatoes are nestled alongside, their golden-brown skin giving way to a creamy harissa aioli that adds a spicy kick. Completing the trio is a tangy cranberry-ginger chutney, its vibrant red hue adding a pop of color to the plate.

Night-Before Prep-Cooking Checklist for Rustic Vineyard Restaurant

  • Prepare the Saffron Rice Pilaf:
    • Measure and rinse 1 cup of uncooked white rice.
    • Mix 2 cups of water with 1/4 teaspoon of saffron threads in a saucepan, heating it over medium heat until the liquid is absorbed.
    • Combine cooked rice with 1 tablespoon of olive oil, salt, and pepper in a large bowl.
  • Prepare the Roasted Sweet Potatoes:
    • Peel and chop 2-3 sweet potatoes into 1-inch cubes.
    • Toss with 2 tablespoons of olive oil, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
    • Spread on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and roast at 400°F (200°C) for 20-25 minutes or until tender.
  • Prepare the Harissa Aioli:
    • Mix 1/2 cup of mayonnaise with 1 tablespoon of harissa paste in a bowl.
    • Add 1 minced garlic clove and 1 tablespoon of freshly squeezed lemon juice to the mixture, stirring well.
  • Prepare the Cranberry-Ginger Chutney:
    • Mix 1 cup of fresh or frozen cranberries with 1/4 cup of chopped fresh ginger in a bowl.
    • Add 2 tablespoons of honey and 1 tablespoon of apple cider vinegar to the mixture, stirring well.
  • Prepare the Baked Salmon:
    • Rinse and pat dry 4 salmon fillets (6 ounces each).
    • Season with salt, pepper, and your choice of herbs (e.g., thyme, rosemary).
    • Place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake at 400°F (200°C) for 12-15 minutes or until cooked through.
